# Canic 0.101 Design: Fleet-Authoritative Service Adoption and Publication

Date: 2026-07-23

## Status

- Status: proposed.
- Depends on the completed
  [0.99 App/Fleet identity hard cut](../0.99-app-fleet-identity-and-terminology-hard-cut/0.99-design.md)
  and the completed
  [0.100 Fleet Coordinator and membership-control-plane extraction](../0.100-multi-subnet-fleet-coordinator-and-registry-synchronization/0.100-design.md).
- Implementation begins only after the 0.100 closeout proves one separately
  installed Coordinator, one committed authority descriptor, one canonical
  membership Registry and verified root mirrors.
- Implementation tracker: [status.md](status.md).

## Thesis

0.101 establishes the canonical writable services of a Fleet without moving or
copying their application data.

For each declared Fleet-authoritative role, Canic must:

1. adopt the one eligible existing Canister in place, preserving its principal
   and stable data; or
2. provision one new Canister on the already resolved Authority Subnet;
3. transfer committed registry and management ownership to the Fleet
   Coordinator through a durable one-way handoff;
4. publish the complete committed service set in one Fleet Registry revision;
   and
5. derive and cascade the Fleet Directory from that committed Registry.

0.101 does not move an existing service between physical Subnets. It does not
replicate service data, create writable peers, promote mirrors or implement
failover.

## Release Boundary

| Release | Canonical end state |
| --- | --- |
| 0.99 | One-member Fleet using final App/Fleet vocabulary, collision-resistant Fleet identity, Fleet-scoped authentication and qualified single-Subnet operation |
| 0.100 | Separate Fleet Coordinator, resolved Authority Subnet, durable authority handoff, canonical member Registry, root mirrors and multi-Subnet membership |
| 0.101 | Adopted or provisioned Fleet-authoritative services, Coordinator-owned authority registry and canonical Fleet Directory publication |
| Later | Service relocation, replacement, replicas, data synchronization, promotion and failover |

The release boundary is hard. 0.101 must not reopen Fleet identity, Coordinator
selection, Authority Subnet selection or member lifecycle decisions owned by
0.99 and 0.100.

## Starting State

At the start of 0.101:

- `FleetBinding` is immutable and installed in the Coordinator, roots and
  managed Canisters.
- `FleetCoordinatorBinding` identifies one committed Coordinator and Authority
  Subnet for one authority epoch.
- The Coordinator is the only Fleet membership writer.
- `FleetRegistry` uses the one current schema containing `authority`,
  `revision`, `members` and an empty `authority_services` vector.
- each eligible member root holds one verified durable Fleet Registry mirror;
- the Coordinator retains qualified final inventory/fence evidence or an
  explicit unqualified record for every removed member;
- member roots cascade member-local topology through their existing same-Subnet
  paths;
- the Coordinator does not yet publish Fleet-authoritative service bindings;
  and
- no service has been moved merely because the Coordinator was extracted.

For an existing Fleet upgraded through 0.99 and 0.100, the Authority Subnet is
the original one-member Fleet's physical Subnet. Existing mutable authority
services therefore remain on the correct physical Subnet and can be adopted
without changing their Canister principals or copying their data.

A member root and the Coordinator may be physically co-located on that Subnet.
They remain distinct Canisters with disjoint logical registries:

- the member root owns member-local Canisters; and
- the Coordinator owns Fleet-authoritative Canisters.

Physical co-location never merges those ownership scopes.

## Goals

1. Introduce one explicit configuration authority for Fleet-authoritative
   service roles.
2. Require every selected role to be singleton, package-backed and eligible for
   Fleet authority.
3. Resolve the complete desired service set before any mutation.
4. Adopt eligible existing mutable services without changing their principals,
   stable data or application identity.
5. Provision missing services only on the resolved Authority Subnet.
6. Transfer service ownership without ever allowing two committed Canic
   registry owners for one service and ownership epoch.
7. Make handoff and provisioning restart-safe and receipt-driven.
8. Keep the Coordinator's operational authority registry separate from the
   public Fleet Registry.
9. Publish only a complete committed service set.
10. Derive Fleet Directory contents from committed Fleet Registry evidence.
11. Preserve local, query-safe authorization; Directory lookup must not become
    an authentication dependency.
12. Fail closed on ambiguous candidates, controller drift, module mismatch,
    unknown creation identity, stale evidence or restore rollback.
13. Qualify every removed member through Coordinator-retained final inventory
    and root-fence evidence before proving that a selected role has no
    duplicate candidate.

## Non-Goals

0.101 does not implement:

- movement of an existing Canister to another physical Subnet;
- application-data export, import, replay or transformation;
- authority-service replicas on member Subnets;
- multiple writable instances of one Fleet-authoritative role;
- mirror promotion, Coordinator promotion or automatic failover;
- consensus, quorum or root-to-root coordination;
- dynamic addition, removal or replacement of authority-service roles after
  the initial set is committed;
- ordinary application-service placement on member Subnets;
- deletion or decommissioning of superseded services;
- a general controller-governance redesign;
- a new delegation or token format;
- a new member lifecycle;
- an application-schema migration; or
- an application upgrade hidden inside the ownership handoff.

An existing service must already run a 0.101-capable package before its
ownership handoff starts. Any required ordinary WASM upgrade is completed and
verified through the existing source-root path before the service is fenced for
adoption.

## Terminology

### Fleet-authoritative service

The one canonical writable service for a Fleet-wide role. Its logical identity
is the pair:

```text
(FleetKey, CanisterRole)
```

The concrete Canister principal is a committed binding of that logical
identity.

### Authority service declaration

The operator-owned configuration selecting the exact Fleet-authoritative roles.
It is desired configuration, not runtime evidence.

### Authority service registry

The Coordinator-owned operational registry of Canisters in the authority
scope. It contains adoption, provisioning and ownership state. It is distinct
from both member roots' local registries and the published Fleet Registry.

### Fleet Registry

The Coordinator-owned, complete, bounded and versioned public topology
authority. In 0.101 it contains committed members and committed
Fleet-authoritative service bindings. It never exposes staged ownership
records.

### Fleet Directory

A derived discovery projection from one verified committed Fleet Registry
snapshot. It does not allocate identity, decide ownership, authorize calls or
repair topology.

### Adoption

Transfer of an eligible existing Canister from a member root's committed local
registry ownership to the Coordinator's authority registry without changing
the Canister principal or application data.

### Provisioning

Creation or allocation and installation of a missing Fleet-authoritative
service on the resolved Authority Subnet.

### Handoff

A durable, authority-epoch-bound transfer whose source and destination may hold
staged records but may not both be committed owners.

## Core Decisions

### 1. Service declaration replaces Directory-owned authority

Fleet-authoritative roles are declared under a service-owned configuration
section:

```toml
[services.fleet]
roles = ["vault_db", "identity_hub"]
```

`[directories.fleet]` does not select or own authoritative services. Directory
contents are derived after service ownership is committed.

The new section selects existing canonical role/package definitions. It does
not duplicate package names, WASM paths, install arguments, scaling settings
or placement settings already owned elsewhere in Canic configuration.

Configuration is authoring input, not a runtime authority on every root. The
host compiles `[services.fleet]` exactly once for an activation:

1. generate one cryptographically random 32-byte `activation_id` and durably
   journal it before compilation or mutation;
2. resolve the exact protected `FleetBinding`;
3. resolve every selected role to its exact package identity, module hash,
   install-argument hash, controller-policy hash and binding-relevant
   configuration hash;
4. encode the canonical declaration bytes under the frozen rules below;
5. compute one `FleetServiceDeclarationHash`;
6. bind the bytes and hash to that activation's host journal; and
7. install the exact bytes and hash in the Coordinator as the `Prepared`
   declaration state.

The Coordinator state is:

```rust
pub enum FleetServiceDeclarationState {
    Absent,
    Prepared {
        activation_id: [u8; 32],
        canonical_bytes: Vec<u8>,
        declaration_hash: FleetServiceDeclarationHash,
    },
    Committed {
        activation_id: [u8; 32],
        canonical_bytes: Vec<u8>,
        declaration_hash: FleetServiceDeclarationHash,
        registry_version: FleetRegistryVersion,
    },
}
```

The bytes are bounded by
`MAX_FLEET_SERVICE_ACTIVATION_PLAN_BYTES`; the committed empty declaration
records the already-current Registry version.

The Coordinator verifies the host's Fleet-administration authorization and
stores the protected prepared declaration as the sole runtime declaration
authority. Roots receive the exact protected bytes/hash through coordinated
upgrade or activation input and compare them with the Coordinator-owned value;
they do not parse local `canic.toml` or independently reinterpret
configuration as runtime authority.

The selected role set is:

- sorted canonically;
- duplicate-free;
- bounded;
- immutable for the duration of activation; and
- hashed with the exact package and binding-relevant configuration selected for
  each role.

The resulting `FleetServiceDeclarationHash` binds planning, handoff receipts
and final publication to one exact desired set.

Before the activation journal commits, changed canonical declaration bytes may
abandon an entirely unused plan and produce a new plan. After the first
Canister allocation or ownership mutation, the prepared declaration becomes
immutable for that activation. After complete publication, the Coordinator
stores it as `Committed`: repeating the exact bytes/hash is
idempotent, while changing, adding or removing a declared role fails closed and
cannot start a second activation. Such evolution belongs to a later
replacement design.

An empty declaration is a valid zero-service Fleet. After complete inventory
qualification, the Coordinator commits the empty declaration idempotently,
keeps `authority_services` and Directory `entries` empty and consumes no
Registry revision because that exact empty set is already current from 0.100.

Once a root installs the protected declaration, it rejects creation,
attachment or ordinary placement of a new member-local Canister for a selected
Fleet-authoritative role. An existing local candidate remains manageable only
for its required ordinary 0.101-capable upgrade and subsequent adoption
protocol. This prevents a new duplicate from appearing after inventory was
collected.

### 2. Only eligible singleton roles may be selected

A selected role must:

- be package-backed and installable;
- be explicitly classified as Fleet-authoritative;
- have exactly one instance per Fleet;
- reject scaling, sharding, keyed bindings and repeatable placement;
- reject member-local placement;
- reject selection of root, Coordinator or framework-infrastructure roles; and
- resolve to one exact expected package/module identity for preflight.

Role lookup is therefore unambiguous. No new `FleetServiceId` is introduced in
0.101: `(FleetKey, CanisterRole)` is the stable logical identity and the
committed principal is its concrete binding.

### 3. Resolution is complete and read-only before mutation

The Coordinator builds one bounded activation plan for the complete declared
role set.

Each `Joining`, `Active` and `Draining` member root exposes one bounded,
authenticated, Coordinator-callable inventory projection for the selected
roles. The projection is derived from that root's committed local registry and
contains the exact role, principal, ownership state and binding evidence
required for planning. It is an administrative read transported through an
inter-Canister update call; it does not create a root mesh or a general
registry replication path.

The Coordinator obtains a current live inventory from every such root before
committing the plan. For every `Removed` member, it instead loads the complete
Coordinator-retained `RemovedMemberQualification` created by 0.100. The
removed root is not called and need not remain live.

Planning fails closed when:

- any `Removed` member is `Unqualified`, has no qualification record or has
  mismatched authority/member/version/hash/fence evidence;
- qualified final inventory is missing, truncated, non-canonical or exceeds
  its frozen 0.100 bound; or
- a selected role appears in a removed member's final inventory with any
  disposition other than `Decommissioned`.

A removed-member record is never silently treated as empty. A possibly live
removed-root candidate blocks provisioning; 0.101 neither adopts it nor moves
it. Operators must resolve the 0.100 qualification/decommissioning evidence
before planning can continue.

Only a live candidate in the member root physically co-located with the
Authority Subnet may be adopted. Candidates elsewhere cause a conflict rather
than relocation. `Removed` roots are never adoption sources.

For every role, resolution produces exactly one of:

```rust
pub enum FleetServicePlanKind {
    Adopt {
        source_root: Principal,
        canister_id: Principal,
    },
    Provision,
}
```

An existing candidate is adoptable only when:

- it appears as one committed entry in the member root physically co-located
  with the Authority Subnet;
- no other live member inventory or qualified removed-member final inventory
  contains an unresolved candidate for the same role;
- its protected `FleetBinding` matches exactly;
- its role and Canister principal match the registry evidence;
- its qualified placement evidence binds it to the Authority Subnet;
- its installed module hash matches the selected 0.101-capable package;
- its stable service-authority state is locally owned by the expected source
  root;
- the source root is an active member authorized by the committed authority
  descriptor;
- its controller state can be transferred safely; and
- no earlier unresolved ownership handoff exists.

Resolution fails before mutation if a role has:

- more than one candidate;
- a candidate on a different physical Subnet;
- a candidate owned by an unexpected root;
- incompatible package, binding or controller evidence; or
- stale or internally inconsistent registry evidence.

Zero eligible candidates means `Provision`. It never means "choose the closest
match" or create a replacement for an ambiguous existing service.

### 3a. Placement evidence is qualified, not discovered

Canic verifies placement as far as the IC exposes it. It does not claim
cryptographic Subnet discovery when the platform supplies no such proof.

For an adopted service, qualified placement evidence is the exact conjunction
of:

- the protected source-root registry row and local Registry content hash;
- the source root/member identity already qualified by the 0.100
  `FleetRegistryAuthority`;
- the trusted deployment, allocation or creation receipt that originally
  bound the principal to that member Subnet;
- the protected service `FleetBinding`, role and parent-root state; and
- any management-canister status evidence the platform currently exposes.

The source member's `SubnetId` must equal the committed Authority Subnet.
Display names, gateway observations and ambient environment configuration are
not evidence.

For a newly provisioned service, qualified placement evidence requires all of:

- a creation request explicitly targeted to the committed Authority Subnet;
- the durable activation journal entry containing that exact request and
  returned principal, or an immutable reference to the same fields in the
  original preallocation journal;
- the successful management-call result and returned Canister principal;
- protected installation evidence binding the principal, role, Fleet and
  Coordinator;
- the trusted deployment/configuration binding used by 0.100; and
- compatible management-canister status evidence when available.

An unknown creation result is not placement evidence. Missing, contradictory
or cross-network evidence fails closed. No candidate is adopted and no
provisioned service becomes committed merely because its principal is
reachable.

### 4. Existing services are adopted in place

Adoption preserves:

- the Canister principal;
- stable memory and application data;
- the service's logical role;
- the installed application package verified during preflight; and
- all non-Canic controllers explicitly allowed by the existing controller
  policy.

Adoption changes:

- committed registry ownership from the source member root to the Coordinator;
- the Canic management controller from the source root to the Coordinator;
- protected service-authority state from local-root ownership to
  Fleet-Coordinator ownership; and
- Directory provenance after final publication.

Adoption is not a data migration. Data-plane endpoints may remain available
during the handoff. Canic-owned lifecycle, upgrade, controller, topology and
other control-plane mutations are fenced from the moment the source enters
`MovingOut` until the destination commit completes.

### 5. Missing services are provisioned on the Authority Subnet

Provisioning reuses the existing Canic package, allocation/pool, install and
receipt machinery. 0.101 must not introduce a second generic installer.

For each missing role:

1. obtain or create a Canister principal on the exact Authority Subnet;
2. durably record that principal before any later step;
3. install the exact selected package and arguments;
4. install the exact `FleetBinding` and service-authority binding;
5. set the Coordinator as the Canic management controller;
6. verify the qualified placement evidence above, Canister status, module hash,
   controllers and protected binding;
7. commit the Canister in the authority service registry; and
8. keep it unpublished until the complete declared set is ready.

If a create operation may have succeeded but the resulting Canister principal
is unknown, the operation becomes `CommandOutcomeUnknown`. Canic must not issue
another create call. Prefer an existing preallocated/pool Canister whose
principal is durably known before activation.

Planning-time capacity checks are advisory. The allocation/create result and
post-create qualified placement evidence are authoritative.

### 6. Ownership handoff is staged and one-way

There is no atomic transaction across a member root, an authority service, the
management Canister, the Coordinator and host state.

Adoption therefore uses one `ServiceHandoffId` bound to:

```text
FleetBinding
authority epoch
FleetServiceDeclarationHash
role
service Canister principal
source root principal
Coordinator principal
Authority Subnet
expected module hash
```

The ID is the domain-separated hash of the canonical encoding of those fields.

The source registry states are:

```rust
pub enum SourceServiceOwnership {
    Committed,
    MovingOut {
        handoff_id: ServiceHandoffId,
        coordinator: Principal,
    },
    Released {
        handoff_id: ServiceHandoffId,
        release_receipt_hash: Hash,
    },
}
```

The Coordinator authority-registry states are:

```rust
pub enum AuthorityServiceOwnership {
    Planned {
        handoff_id: ServiceHandoffId,
    },
    PreparedIn {
        handoff_id: ServiceHandoffId,
        source_root: Principal,
        evidence_hash: Hash,
    },
    Committed {
        handoff_id: ServiceHandoffId,
        binding: FleetServiceAuthorityBinding,
    },
}
```

The source stops being a committed owner when it durably enters `MovingOut`.
The Coordinator cannot become committed until the source has durably entered
`Released`. A period with no committed owner is permitted. Two committed owners
are not.

After the source enters `MovingOut`, cancellation and rollback to
source-committed ownership are forbidden in 0.101. Recovery resumes the same
handoff forward.

### 7. Controller transfer uses exact-set reconciliation

For an adopted service, the source root performs one management update that:

- removes the source root as a Canic controller;
- adds the exact committed Coordinator principal; and
- preserves the exact preflight-approved non-Canic controller set.

The expected after-set is:

```text
(approved controllers - source root) + Coordinator
```

It must never be empty.

Before the call, the source rechecks that the controller set still equals the
planned before-set. Drift aborts the step without mutation.

If the management-call result is unknown:

- the source queries status if it is still a controller;
- the Coordinator queries status if it became a controller;
- exact before-state permits a bounded retry of the same operation;
- exact after-state permits forward reconciliation; and
- any third state fails closed as `ControllerStateConflict`.

No retry is allowed merely because a receipt is missing.

The Canic committed-owner invariant governs Canic control-plane authority.
Explicitly preserved external controllers remain outside that invariant and
retain whatever direct IC authority the operator already granted them.

### 8. Each authority service stores a protected ownership binding

Every adopted or provisioned service stores:

```rust
pub struct FleetServiceAuthorityBinding {
    pub fleet: FleetBinding,
    pub role: CanisterRole,
    pub service_canister_id: Principal,
    pub coordinator: Principal,
    pub authority_subnet: SubnetId,
    pub authority_epoch: u64,
    pub declaration_hash: FleetServiceDeclarationHash,
}
```

The service-local state is:

```rust
pub enum ServiceAuthorityState {
    Local {
        fleet: FleetBinding,
        parent_root: Principal,
    },
    Preparing {
        handoff_id: ServiceHandoffId,
        binding: FleetServiceAuthorityBinding,
    },
    Provisioning {
        plan_hash: Hash,
        binding: FleetServiceAuthorityBinding,
    },
    Fleet {
        binding: FleetServiceAuthorityBinding,
    },
}
```

An existing 0.99/0.100 service migrates to `Local` on its 0.101-capable ordinary
upgrade.

Only the current local parent root may move `Local` to `Preparing`. Only the
exact Coordinator named in the prepared binding may move `Preparing` to
`Fleet`, and only after a destination-commit receipt is verified.

A newly installed service starts in `Provisioning`, not `Fleet`. Only the exact
Coordinator may move it to `Fleet`, and only after its authority-registry
ownership is committed.

While `Preparing` or `Provisioning`, Canic control-plane mutation is fenced.
Business/data-plane behaviour of an adopted service remains unchanged unless
the application itself requires a stricter quiescence policy. A newly
provisioned service is not externally published until it reaches `Fleet`.

Neither a Directory entry nor IC controller status alone may rewrite this
protected binding.

### 9. Publication is complete and atomic at the Fleet Registry boundary

0.101 reuses the exact current Fleet Registry schema established by 0.100.
The field already exists and begins empty:

```rust
pub struct FleetRegistry {
    pub authority: FleetRegistryAuthority,
    pub revision: u64,
    pub members: Vec<FleetRegistryEntry>,
    pub authority_services: Vec<FleetAuthorityServiceBinding>,
}

pub struct FleetAuthorityServiceBinding {
    pub role: CanisterRole,
    pub canister_id: Principal,
}
```

`authority_services` contains committed bindings only. Planning, provisioning,
`MovingOut` and `PreparedIn` state never appears in the public snapshot.
`FleetRegistry.authority.binding` is the only stored `FleetBinding` in the
public Registry hierarchy. The public per-service binding does not repeat the
Fleet, Coordinator, Authority Subnet or authority epoch.

Bindings are canonically ordered by role. Duplicate roles or Canister
principals are rejected. Every binding inherits the exact snapshot Fleet and
authority descriptor.

The Coordinator publishes the complete declared service set in one checked
revision increment only after:

- every `Provision` entry is committed in the authority registry;
- every `Adopt` entry has a durable source `Released` receipt;
- every service stores the exact committed `FleetServiceAuthorityBinding`;
- every service's current status matches its final controller, module and
  qualified placement evidence; and
- the resulting role set equals the declaration exactly.

Partial service sets are never canonical.

After publication, removing or replacing a service binding is rejected in
0.101 and requires a later design.

### 10. Fleet Directory is a pure projection

0.101 uses the exact 0.100 public DTOs:

```rust
pub struct FleetDirectory {
    pub provenance: DirectoryProvenance,
    pub entries: Vec<DirectoryEntryInput>,
    pub subnets: Vec<FleetDirectorySubnet>,
}

pub struct DirectoryProvenance {
    pub authority: FleetRegistryAuthority,
    pub source_root: Principal,
    pub registry_revision: u64,
    pub registry_content_hash: [u8; 32],
    pub synchronized_at: Timestamp,
}

pub struct FleetDirectorySubnet {
    pub subnet: SubnetId,
    pub root: Principal,
    pub status: ManagedSubnetStatus,
}
```

For one verified Fleet Registry snapshot:

```text
authority_services.role + authority_services.canister_id
    -> entries

members
    -> subnets
```

Committed `authority_services` populate the existing `entries` field.
`subnets` remains the exact complete 0.100 membership projection. No
service-only Directory, companion provenance or second cascade field is
introduced. The exact `DirectoryProvenance` above remains the public
provenance contract.

Roots accept a Fleet Directory projection only from their locally verified
current Fleet Registry mirror. They do not accept an independently pushed list
of service principals.

Each eligible root:

1. synchronizes the complete Fleet Registry through the 0.100 protocol;
2. verifies its binding, authority, revision, canonical bytes and hash;
3. derives the complete Fleet Directory;
4. atomically activates its durable mirror and local Directory snapshot with
   one `synchronized_at`; and
5. cascades the exact projection through existing same-Subnet paths.

`synchronized_at` is exactly the time the root atomically activates the
verified Registry mirror and its derived Fleet Directory for that Registry
revision. It is not the Coordinator publication time, last polling time, last
confirmation time or local cascade-completion time. An unchanged head check
does not modify it or cause a cascade.

Before the complete declared service set is committed in one Registry
revision, `entries` remains empty. Planning, `MovingOut`, `PreparedIn`,
provisioned-but-unpublished and partial ready sets never populate it.

### 11. Activation freezes membership mutation, not synchronization

From activation-plan commitment until Fleet Registry service publication, the
Coordinator rejects member join, drain, remove and other Registry mutations
with `FleetServiceActivationInProgress`.

Existing roots may continue polling and serving the current membership
snapshot. Business traffic and member-local topology remain available.

Freezing membership mutation prevents the activation plan, authority evidence
and final publication base from drifting across independent Registry changes.
Membership mutation resumes immediately after service publication.

### 12. Recovery is forward-only after the first fence

The Coordinator owns one durable activation journal:

```rust
pub enum FleetServiceActivationState {
    Unconfigured,
    Planned {
        plan_hash: Hash,
        declaration_hash: FleetServiceDeclarationHash,
        base_registry_version: FleetRegistryVersion,
    },
    Executing {
        plan_hash: Hash,
    },
    ReadyToPublish {
        plan_hash: Hash,
    },
    Published {
        registry_version: FleetRegistryVersion,
    },
}
```

Every service has a durable operation record containing:

- plan kind;
- expected identities and hashes;
- current phase;
- management-call request identity;
- exact observed before/after evidence;
- durable receipts; and
- typed failure state.

Before `Executing`, an unmutated plan may be abandoned and rebuilt. After the
first source enters `MovingOut` or the first new Canister is allocated for the
plan, the plan is forward-only.

The host may start, inspect and resume the workflow, but host files are not
ownership authority. Loss of host state must not permit a second plan or a
second owner.

## Adoption Protocol

For each planned adoption, execute the following durable sequence:

1. **Revalidate plan**
   - Confirm the current Fleet binding, authority descriptor, declaration hash,
     Registry base and source candidate evidence.
   - Confirm membership mutation remains fenced.

2. **Fence the source**
   - The source root atomically changes its local registry entry from
     `Committed` to `MovingOut(handoff_id)`.
   - All source-root Canic control-plane operations for that service now reject.
   - Persist and return a `MovingOutReceipt`.

3. **Prepare the service**
   - The source root calls the service's structural prepare endpoint.
   - The service verifies caller, local ownership, Fleet binding, role,
     principal and handoff fields.
   - The service persists `ServiceAuthorityState::Preparing`.
   - Repeated exact calls return the same receipt.

4. **Transfer controller state**
   - Recheck the exact planned controller before-set.
   - Replace it with the exact approved after-set.
   - Reconcile unknown outcomes through exact status evidence.

5. **Prepare the destination**
   - The Coordinator verifies controller ownership, module hash,
     running/stopped status policy, qualified placement evidence and service
     prepare receipt.
   - It persists `AuthorityServiceOwnership::PreparedIn`.

6. **Release the source**
   - The Coordinator presents exact `PreparedIn` evidence to the source root.
   - The source verifies the handoff and persists `Released`.
   - The source returns an immutable release receipt and retains a tombstone.

7. **Commit destination ownership**
   - The Coordinator rechecks the service-local binding and final status.
   - It changes the authority-registry record from `PreparedIn` to `Committed`.
   - It persists and returns an immutable destination-commit receipt.

8. **Commit service-local authority**
   - The Coordinator calls the prepared service.
   - The service verifies the destination-commit receipt and exact Coordinator
     caller.
   - It persists `ServiceAuthorityState::Fleet`.

9. **Mark service ready**
   - The activation journal records the final binding and evidence hashes.
   - The service remains unpublished until every declared role is ready.

If execution stops after step 2, it resumes forward at the first incomplete
step. It must never restore source `Committed` ownership.

## Provisioning Protocol

Provision missing roles before fencing existing adopted services where
possible. This reduces the time an existing service spends in a forward-only
handoff.

For each planned provision:

1. resolve the exact package, install arguments, controller policy and Authority
   Subnet;
2. obtain a durably known Canister principal;
3. verify or create the Canister on the Authority Subnet;
4. install the package with `ServiceAuthorityState::Provisioning` binding
   inputs;
5. verify module, controller, qualified placement, role and Fleet binding
   evidence;
6. insert one committed authority-registry record; and
7. activate the service-local state to `Fleet` using the destination-commit
   receipt;
8. retain the service as ready but unpublished.

Provisioning is idempotent only from a known Canister principal and exact
evidence. An unknown create result is not idempotent and must not be retried.

If a provisioned service is committed but final publication has not occurred,
recovery retains and reuses that exact principal. It does not create another
instance.

## Planning Rules

The activation plan contains the complete declared role set and no others.

It must be rejected when:

- 0.99 or 0.100 qualification evidence is absent;
- the Coordinator is not the committed authority for the current epoch;
- the Coordinator Registry or any required source registry is unhealthy;
- any removed member lacks qualified final inventory/root-fence evidence;
- a selected role remains non-decommissioned in qualified removed-member
  evidence;
- a selected role is ineligible or unbounded;
- membership mutation or another activation is already in progress;
- a selected role resolves to ambiguous or misplaced candidates;
- an adoption candidate is not running a 0.101-capable package;
- controller transfer would produce a forbidden controller set;
- a stale handoff or release tombstone conflicts with the plan;
- the current Fleet Registry already publishes a different service set; or
- any plan or snapshot bound would be exceeded.

The plan is canonical and hashable. Reordering configuration or source
inventory must not change its hash.

## Current-Schema Ordered Upgrade

0.101 does not introduce a Registry protocol generation or change the
`FleetRegistry` wire shape. The current 0.100 schema already contains the
empty `authority_services` field.

The ordered upgrade is:

1. qualify 0.100 and capture the exact current Registry version, hash,
   qualified/unqualified removed-member evidence and empty
   `authority_services`;
2. upgrade existing selected authority services to 0.101-capable packages
   through their current source roots;
3. upgrade member roots in the established order, perform their ordinary
   one-time local stable-state migration and place Fleet synchronization in
   neutral `AwaitingCoordinatorUpgrade` where a local fence is needed;
4. while fenced, roots retain the last verified mirror and Directory but do
   not accept a Coordinator response whose current contract cannot be
   verified;
5. upgrade the Coordinator last without changing its principal, authority
   epoch or current Fleet Registry bytes;
6. reconcile every root's exact protected declaration hash and resume current
   Registry synchronization;
7. require every non-removed root to have the 0.101 code and protected
   declaration before the deployment is qualified; a temporarily unavailable
   already-upgraded root may synchronize the unchanged current schema later;
   and
8. begin the service activation plan.

There is one decoder, one wire contract and no runtime schema selector. A
one-time local stable migration may read the immediately preceding
local storage layout during upgrade and must rewrite it before activation; no
fallback reader remains.

No Fleet Registry revision is consumed for the code or local stable-state
upgrade. The one checked revision increment occurs only when the complete
declared authority-service set is published.

## Fresh-Fleet and Existing-Fleet Paths

### Existing Fleet

- Preserve the 0.100 Authority Subnet.
- Qualify every live member inventory and every Coordinator-retained removed
  member final inventory/fence before resolving roles.
- Search only the co-located member root's committed local registry for
  adoptable existing services.
- Adopt matching services in place.
- Provision only roles with no existing candidate anywhere in allowed
  inventory.
- Reject an existing candidate on another Subnet; relocation is later work.

### Fresh Fleet

- Use the Authority Subnet already resolved by 0.100.
- Require the same complete live/removed-member duplicate proof if membership
  changed before service activation.
- Provision every declared service.
- The Coordinator is the initial committed owner.
- No synthetic source root or handoff is created.

### Mixed Fleet

A bounded mix of adoption and provisioning is allowed in one activation plan.
All missing services are provisioned and verified first where possible, then
existing services are handed off, and the complete set is published once.

## Authorization

0.101 preserves the 0.99 Fleet-scoped trust boundary and the 0.100 committed
Coordinator identity.

- Operator activation endpoints require the existing Fleet-administration
  capability for the exact `FleetBinding`.
- Coordinator-to-root adoption calls require the exact committed Coordinator
  principal, authority epoch and structural capability.
- Source-root-to-service prepare calls require the exact locally stored parent
  root.
- Coordinator-to-service commit calls require the exact Coordinator stored in
  the prepared binding.
- Root snapshot synchronization continues to use the 0.100 root membership and
  caller rules.
- Directory entries are never used as issuer, administrator or caller
  authority.
- IC controller status alone never grants Fleet-administration authority.
- Authentication verification remains local and query-safe; it must not call a
  Directory, Registry, root or Coordinator.

Replay and idempotency keys bind:

```text
operation variant
FleetBinding
authority epoch
declaration hash
handoff or plan ID
target service principal
canonical payload hash
caller principal
request ID
```

## Stable State and Backup

The following are durable:

- prepared/committed canonical service declaration bytes and hash;
- canonical activation plan bytes and hash;
- per-service activation phases and evidence hashes;
- source `MovingOut` and `Released` states;
- destination `PreparedIn` and `Committed` states;
- service-local `Preparing` and `Fleet` bindings;
- controller before/after evidence;
- adopted and provisioned qualified placement evidence;
- allocation, create and install receipts;
- every 0.100 removed-member qualification record consumed by planning;
- final Fleet Registry version and canonical bytes;
- root mirrors using the one current Registry schema;
- Directory projection provenance; and
- typed unknown-outcome state.

Backups and restores must preserve these relationships.

A restored former source root must not reactivate a pre-handoff committed entry
from an old backup. It remains control-plane fenced until it synchronizes with
the current Coordinator and reconciles the service's current authority epoch,
Fleet Registry binding and controller state.

An old Coordinator backup cannot regain mutation authority at the same
authority epoch unless it proves equality with the latest committed Registry
revision and authority-service state. Otherwise it fails closed pending a
later epoch-advancing recovery design.

A root mirror, Fleet Directory or host receipt cannot promote itself into
ownership authority.

## Failure and Recovery Rules

### Before execution

- A read-only plan may be replaced if no mutation or allocation occurred.
- Preflight failure changes no runtime authority.

### After provisioning begins

- A known allocated principal is retained and reconciled.
- An unknown creation identity becomes `CommandOutcomeUnknown`.
- A committed provisioned service is reused on resume.

### After a source enters `MovingOut`

- rollback to source `Committed` is forbidden;
- the source remains control-plane fenced;
- exact repeated requests return the stored receipt;
- controller outcome is reconciled before any retry;
- the destination resumes the same handoff ID; and
- operators may repair a typed external precondition but may not create a
  second plan.

### After Fleet Registry publication

- the published service set is canonical;
- loss of a Directory cascade does not roll back the Registry;
- roots retry synchronization and local cascade;
- membership mutation may resume; and
- 0.101 provides no removal or replacement path.

## Bounds and Canonical Encoding

0.101 reuses the 0.100 Registry constants without alteration:

```rust
const GENESIS_AUTHORITY_EPOCH: u64 = 1;
const GENESIS_REGISTRY_REVISION: u64 = 1;
const MAX_MANAGED_SUBNETS: usize = 256;
const MAX_FLEET_REGISTRY_SNAPSHOT_BYTES: usize = 131_072;
const MAX_REMOVED_MEMBER_INVENTORY_ENTRIES: usize = 4_096;
const MAX_REMOVED_MEMBER_INVENTORY_BYTES: usize = 1_048_576;

const MIN_POLL_INTERVAL_SECONDS: u64 = 60;
const DEFAULT_POLL_INTERVAL_SECONDS: u64 = 300;
const MAX_POLL_INTERVAL_SECONDS: u64 = 3_600;
const INITIAL_RETRY_BACKOFF_SECONDS: u64 = 15;
const MAX_RETRY_BACKOFF_SECONDS: u64 = 900;
const MAX_SUPERSEDED_RETRIES_PER_PASS: u8 = 3;
```

The 0.101-specific bounds are:

```rust
const MAX_FLEET_AUTHORITY_SERVICES: usize = 32;
const MAX_FLEET_SERVICE_ACTIVATION_PLAN_BYTES: usize = 16_384;
const MAX_FLEET_SERVICE_CONTROLLERS: usize = 10;
const MAX_RETAINED_SERVICE_EVIDENCE_BYTES_PER_ROLE: usize = 65_536;
const MAX_RETAINED_SERVICE_RECEIPT_BYTES_PER_ROLE: usize = 32_768;
const MAX_SINGLE_SERVICE_RECEIPT_BYTES: usize = 4_096;
```

The role limit counts every declared role. The activation-plan limit applies
to the exact canonical plan bytes, including the declaration hash and all
per-role plan/evidence hashes. The controller bound applies to both the
before-set and after-set and matches the IC platform maximum; a lower
package-specific controller-policy bound still wins. The platform value is
defined by the
[IC Canister settings reference](https://docs.internetcomputer.org/guides/canister-management/settings/).
Evidence and receipt bounds apply to retained canonical bytes per role, not
merely human-readable projections. Exceeding any bound fails before allocation,
ownership mutation or Registry publication. Exact duplicate receipts are
retained once by hash; each phase reserves its maximum receipt capacity before
any side effect. Insufficient retained capacity fails before that phase begins.

0.101 uses the deterministic CBOR rules frozen by 0.100: records are fixed
arrays in documented field order; integers use shortest encoding; principals,
IDs and digests are byte strings; vectors are definite-length; and maps, text
keys, floats and indefinite-length items are forbidden. Nested
`FleetBinding`, `FleetRegistryAuthority` and `FleetRegistryVersion` values use
their already frozen raw encodings.

Canonical ordering is exact:

- declaration and plan entries: raw `CanisterRole` bytes ascending;
- public `authority_services`: raw role bytes ascending;
- source and removed-member inventories: raw Canister principal bytes, then
  raw role bytes ascending;
- controller sets: raw principal bytes ascending; and
- receipts retained for one role: operation-phase discriminant ascending, then
  receipt hash bytes ascending.

The declaration bytes are one fixed array containing, in order:

```text
FleetBinding
activation_id
[
  role,
  package_identity,
  module_hash,
  install_arguments_hash,
  controller_policy_hash,
  binding_relevant_configuration_hash
]
```

The role arrays are canonically sorted. The activation-plan bytes are one
fixed array containing, in order:

```text
activation_id
FleetRegistryVersion
FleetServiceDeclarationHash
[
  role,
  plan_kind_discriminant,
  source_root_or_empty,
  canister_principal_or_empty,
  package_identity,
  module_hash,
  placement_basis_hash,
  controller_before_evidence_hash_or_empty
]
```

`Adopt` uses plan-kind discriminant `0`; `Provision` uses `1`. For `Adopt`,
`placement_basis_hash` is the qualified adopted-placement evidence hash. For
`Provision`, it is the canonical explicitly Authority-Subnet-targeted creation
request hash; the returned principal and post-creation placement evidence do
not exist at plan time and are retained separately when observed. Empty
optional values encode as CBOR null. The handoff bytes use the exact field
order already listed in “Ownership handoff is staged and one-way”:
`FleetBinding`, authority epoch, declaration hash, role, service principal,
source root, Coordinator, Authority Subnet and expected module hash.

The canonical placement and controller evidence arrays are:

```text
adopted placement:
[
  FleetRegistryAuthority,
  source_member_identity,
  source_registry_version,
  source_registry_content_hash,
  source_registry_row_hash,
  trusted_deployment_or_creation_receipt_hash,
  protected_service_binding_hash,
  management_status_evidence_hash_or_empty
]

targeted creation request:
[
  FleetRegistryAuthority,
  activation_id,
  FleetServiceDeclarationHash,
  role,
  AuthoritySubnetId,
  package_identity,
  module_hash,
  install_arguments_hash,
  controller_policy_hash
]

provisioned placement:
[
  FleetRegistryAuthority,
  targeted_creation_request_hash,
  management_call_result_hash,
  returned_canister_principal,
  protected_installation_evidence_hash,
  trusted_deployment_configuration_binding_hash,
  management_status_evidence_hash_or_empty
]

controller set:
[
  FleetRegistryAuthority,
  role,
  service_canister_principal,
  observation_phase_discriminant,
  observed_at,
  [controller_principals]
]
```

The `controller_principals` array uses the canonical principal ordering above.
`observation_phase_discriminant` is `0 = planned-before`,
`1 = reconciled-before` or `2 = reconciled-after`. Optional status evidence
uses CBOR null when the platform does not expose it. The named hashes inside
these arrays are hashes of existing protected canonical objects or receipts;
they are never reconstructed from display projections.

Every evidence object and receipt hash uses:

```text
SHA-256(
  domain_separator
  || u64_be(canonical_byte_length)
  || exact_canonical_bytes
)
```

The domain separators are exactly:

```text
canic:fleet-service-declaration\0
canic:fleet-service-activation-plan\0
canic:fleet-service-handoff\0
canic:fleet-service-evidence:adopted-placement\0
canic:fleet-service-evidence:targeted-creation-request\0
canic:fleet-service-evidence:provisioned-placement\0
canic:fleet-service-evidence:controller-set\0
canic:fleet-service-receipt:moving-out\0
canic:fleet-service-receipt:service-prepared\0
canic:fleet-service-receipt:prepared-in\0
canic:fleet-service-receipt:released\0
canic:fleet-service-receipt:destination-committed\0
canic:fleet-service-receipt:service-active\0
canic:fleet-service-receipt:provisioned\0
```

Each receipt's canonical bytes are the exact persisted fixed-array DTO for
that named phase, including its operation ID, complete authority, declaration
hash, role, target principal, preceding evidence hash, resulting state hash
and timestamp in that order. A receipt omits no identity field merely because
the caller also stores it.

Registry revision increment continues to use checked `u64` arithmetic and
fails as `RevisionExhausted` before mutation. 0.101 does not advance or mutate
the authority epoch. An epoch mismatch fails closed.

Retryable idempotent calls use exponential backoff from 15 seconds through
30, 60, 120, 240, 480 and the 900-second cap. There is no attempt-count cutoff
for an unresolved forward-only handoff; it remains pending with bounded retry
intervals until success or a typed terminal conflict. Administrative
force-retry repeats only the exact persisted request. A non-idempotent create
with unknown principal is never retried. Root Registry polling and
`VersionSuperseded` handling retain the 0.100 limits above.

Unresolved handoffs, source `Released` tombstones, destination ownership
records, service-local bindings and committed per-role evidence are retained
for the lifetime of this protocol. Qualified and unqualified removed-member
evidence follows the 0.100 member-tombstone lifetime rule. Because the role set
is bounded and 0.101 has no replacement loop, no age-, count- or
success-based garbage collection is permitted. A later explicit compaction or
replacement design must supersede this rule before discarding any such
evidence.

Hashes always cover the exact canonical bytes transferred or persisted. No
logical-field reconstruction is an alternative hash authority.

## Invariants

1. One `FleetBinding` identifies the Fleet for every 0.101 object.
2. One committed authority descriptor identifies the Coordinator and Authority
   Subnet for an authority epoch.
3. The authority-service declaration contains only eligible singleton roles.
4. A committed Fleet Registry contains at most one service binding per role.
5. A committed Fleet Registry contains at most one role binding per Canister
   principal.
6. Every published service binding is interpreted only under the enclosing
   snapshot's Fleet, Coordinator, Authority Subnet and authority epoch.
7. An existing adopted service preserves its Canister principal and application
   data.
8. Every newly provisioned service has the complete qualified
   Authority-Subnet-targeted creation and installation evidence; no
   cryptographic Subnet-discovery claim is implied.
9. At steady state, every managed authority service has exactly one committed
   registry owner.
10. During handoff, source and destination may hold staged records, but they are
    never both committed owners.
11. `MovingOut` is a one-way fence in 0.101.
12. A destination commit requires a durable source-release receipt.
13. A source release requires exact destination-preparation and controller
    evidence.
14. A service in `Preparing` accepts no Canic control-plane mutation except
    exact handoff recovery.
15. A service in `Fleet` binds to the exact committed Coordinator and authority
    epoch.
16. No partial authority-service set is published.
17. The published service role set equals the declaration exactly.
18. Fleet Directory contents are a deterministic projection of one verified
    Fleet Registry snapshot.
19. Directory state has no ownership or authentication authority.
20. Membership mutation is fenced while service activation is executing.
21. Missing receipts never justify blind repetition of a non-idempotent
    operation.
22. Old backups cannot lower the committed Registry revision or resurrect
    released ownership.
23. The public Fleet Registry stores `FleetBinding` only through
    `authority.binding`; no top-level or per-service copy competes with it.
24. Every removed member has qualified final inventory and root-fence evidence
    before a plan commits.
25. A selected role recorded as non-decommissioned on a removed member blocks
    both adoption and provisioning.
26. After publication, the committed operational authority-registry role and
    principal set equals both the declaration and public
    `authority_services` set exactly.
27. One prepared/committed declaration is the runtime authority; roots verify
    its bytes/hash and never reinterpret local configuration.
28. `synchronized_at` is the root's atomic mirror-and-Directory activation
    time for the exact Registry revision and no other event.

## Validation

### Configuration and planning

- selected roles are canonical, bounded, unique and eligible;
- scaled, sharded, keyed or member-local roles are rejected;
- configuration reorder does not change declaration or plan hashes;
- roots verify the exact Coordinator-owned declaration bytes/hash and never
  reinterpret local configuration as authority;
- an unused plan may be replaced before allocation or ownership mutation;
- declaration drift after the first mutation or complete publication fails
  closed and cannot start a second activation;
- an empty declaration commits idempotently without a Registry revision;
- duplicate or ambiguous existing candidates fail before mutation;
- every removed member supplies valid qualified final inventory/fence evidence;
- an unqualified removed member or selected non-decommissioned removed-member
  candidate blocks planning and provisioning;
- a candidate on a non-Authority Subnet is rejected;
- a module, binding, role, controller, source-owner or qualified-placement
  mismatch is rejected;
- the exact 32-role, 16,384-byte plan, 10-controller and retained-evidence
  bounds are enforced before mutation; and
- membership mutation is rejected while activation executes.

### Existing mutable-service adoption

Using an IcyDB-backed authority service:

1. write identifiable application data before adoption;
2. record its Canister principal, module hash and stable-state evidence;
3. upgrade it to the 0.101-capable package through the source root;
4. interrupt and resume at every adoption boundary;
5. complete the controller and registry handoff;
6. prove the principal is unchanged;
7. prove the application data is unchanged and remains writable;
8. prove the source registry retains only a released tombstone;
9. prove the Coordinator authority registry is the sole committed owner; and
10. prove the Fleet Directory resolves the same principal.

### Provisioning

- every new service has complete qualified Authority-Subnet placement evidence;
- a targeted creation request without its journal, successful result,
  returned principal, protected installation evidence and trusted deployment
  binding is rejected;
- exact package, install arguments, binding and controllers are verified;
- a known allocation is reused after interruption;
- an unknown create identity fails closed without a second creation;
- mixed adoption/provision plans publish only after every role is ready; and
- a committed but unpublished provisioned service is reused on resume.

### Ownership safety

- no execution trace contains two committed registry owners;
- source `MovingOut` survives restart and cannot roll back;
- destination commit rejects absent or mismatched release evidence;
- controller exact-before permits a safe retry;
- controller exact-after permits forward reconciliation;
- a third controller state fails closed;
- repeated exact handoff calls return the same receipts; and
- conflicting handoff IDs are rejected; and
- unresolved handoffs and committed per-role ownership evidence survive
  restart and are never age- or success-evicted.

### Registry and Directory

- the ordered upgrade retains the one current Registry schema and consumes no
  revision for code or local stable-state migration;
- complete service publication performs one checked revision increment;
- the Fleet Registry has exactly `authority`, `revision`, `members` and
  `authority_services`, with one Fleet binding beneath `authority`;
- the existing 0.100 `FleetAuthorityServiceBinding` is reused without a
  near-duplicate public type;
- partial service sets never appear in a committed snapshot;
- canonical ordering and bytes are stable;
- every root derives the same Directory from the same Registry version;
- `entries` equals the complete committed service set and `subnets` remains
  the complete membership projection;
- `synchronized_at` changes only on atomic activation of the exact mirror and
  derived Directory, never on publication, polling, confirmation or cascade
  completion;
- stale or mismatched snapshots are rejected;
- removed roots cannot synchronize;
- joining, active and draining roots follow the 0.100 access rules; and
- lost local cascades recover without changing canonical Registry state.

### Restore and monotonicity

- restoring a pre-release source backup does not resurrect ownership;
- restoring an old Coordinator backup cannot become writable at the same epoch
  without latest-state proof;
- restoring a root mirror never creates authority;
- host-state loss does not permit a second activation plan;
- restoring or omitting removed-member evidence cannot turn an unqualified
  member into an empty one; and
- all recovery paths preserve the published revision and service bindings.

- all-target Clippy with warnings denied;

   Authority-Subnet placement evidence;

## Expected Follow-On

A later design may introduce member-Subnet replicas of selected
Fleet-authoritative services.

That design must separately define:

- snapshot or log transport;
- replica identity and ownership;
- lag and freshness evidence;
- read routing;
- promotion eligibility;
- fencing of the prior writable instance;
- authority-epoch advancement;
- restore and split-brain prevention; and
- service relocation or replacement.

Nothing in 0.101 implies that a mirror or replica may become writable.

## Finding

`CANIC-096-CAPACITY-008` was a P1 settlement-liveness defect. Application
admission reserved a primary receipt and resource quantity, but a later
terminal transition needed a new ordered-index allocation. At stable-memory
saturation, the already-admitted operation could therefore lack the local
capacity needed to persist its terminal authority.

The hard-cut fix provisions the eligibility allocation before admitting the
pending operation. Once admission succeeds, settlement updates a fixed-size
primary slot and an existing resource-total row after inserting the already
provisioned eligibility entry. It performs no new logical capacity admission.

## Frozen Contract

- Terminal observation grace is exactly 24 hours, equal to the maximum
  admitted replay horizon.
- Eligibility is
  `max(replay_deadline_ns, terminal_timestamp_ns + 24 hours)`.
- Addition is checked before mutation.
- Pending rows never receive terminal eligibility and are never removed due
  to age.
- Allocation 47 is the sole ordered terminal-eligibility authority.
- Its key is the exact 40-byte `(eligible_at_ns, operation_id)` ordering.
- Its value binds schema, operation ID, payload binding, and terminal revision.
- Reclamation and timer registration remain disabled in this slice.

## Capacity Evidence

The pinned `ic-stable-structures 0.7.2` B-tree has minimum degree six, so each
non-root live node contains at least five entries. For allocation 47:

- maximum serialized value: 229 bytes;
- fixed key: 40 bytes;
- node page: 2,362 bytes;
- allocator chunk header: 16 bytes;
- fixed map, allocator, and spare-chunk headers: 116 bytes;
- 100,000-row maximum live-node reservation: 726 Wasm pages;
- measured ascending insertion: 726 pages;
- measured deterministic permutation: 626 pages; and
- four-allocation managed ascending subtotal: 4,737 physical Wasm pages,
  296.0625 MiB.

The reservation formula is intentionally specific to the pinned dependency and
record shape. The explicit ignored capacity probe fails if those assumptions
drift.

## Failure Behavior

- Timestamp overflow rejects before eligibility, primary, or aggregate
  mutation.
- Failure to provision the required stable pages rejects before primary or
  aggregate mutation.
- Existing exact eligibility rejects settlement before primary or aggregate
  mutation.
- Lifecycle validation compares the complete expected ordered projection to
  the stable index before clearing the placement acknowledgement index.
- Missing, extra, misordered, wrong-schema, wrong-identity, wrong-binding, or
  wrong-revision eligibility fails closed.
- No failure path deletes a receipt, eligibility entry, or resource total.

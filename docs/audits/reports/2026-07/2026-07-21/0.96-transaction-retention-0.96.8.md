## Finding

The released one-day replay ceiling and one-day terminal observation grace
were based on a downstream checkout now known to be approximately one month
stale. That evidence cannot define the current downstream transaction window.
Making a resolved crypto action wait on day-scale eligibility also consumes the
fixed 1,000-row receipt budget long after the likely user retry interval.

This finding affects only the Canic-owned timing policy. It does not reopen the
stable schema, cleanup owner, capacity ceiling, receipt authority, or
downstream implementation.

## Hard-Cut Decision

```text
maximum remaining replay window = 1 hour
terminal observation grace      = 15 minutes
terminal eligible at            = max(replay deadline, terminal time + grace)
maximum span when resolved by deadline = 75 minutes from admission
```

The one-hour value is an inclusive ceiling rather than a default. A trusted
adapter can and should supply a shorter immutable action or authorization
deadline when its domain contract supports one. An absent request at the
deadline remains closed, and a request one nanosecond beyond the ceiling
rejects before capacity or resource mutation.

No 24-hour mode, configuration override, alias, fallback, or compatibility
reader remains.

Terminal eligibility keys persist the exact policy result and lifecycle
validation does not rewrite them. Before upgrading, guarded receipt-capacity
status must report `terminal_application_receipt_records = 0`. Pending rows
remain valid and retain their immutable deadlines; settlement after upgrade
creates eligibility under the new rule. An installation that cannot drain
terminal rows under the old release must reinstall/reset its pre-1.0
application state. This is an explicit operational hard cut, not an automatic
migration.

## Failure And Ambiguity Boundary

A definitive transaction failure should settle the receipt `RolledBack`,
which releases its reservation and schedules the terminal receipt for bounded
reclamation. A definitive success settles `Committed` and preserves resource
accounting after receipt removal.

An IC call failure does not necessarily prove the external effect failed.
Those unknown outcomes remain `Pending` until authoritative downstream
recovery determines success or no-effect. The hard cut does not age-delete
pending records, infer rollback from elapsed time, or trade duplicate-effect
safety for capacity.

## Capacity Consequence

Receipts resolved no later than the replay deadline are now reclaimable within
75 minutes of admission under the maximum window. A later authoritative
recovery remains pending for as long as necessary, then retains its terminal
result for 15 minutes. The exact cleanup timer and shared 32-item batch are
unchanged and wake at the first durable eligibility deadline rather than
polling. The 1,000-row receipt and resource-total ceilings are unchanged.

The maintainer's current planning bound is approximately ten collections per
project instance. One stable resource identity per collection therefore uses
about one percent of the separate 1,000-resource ceiling. Per-operation,
per-user, per-token, or per-mint resource keys would violate that intended
cardinality model and remain a downstream adoption error.

## Contract Impact

- The two Canic-owned policy constants change to one hour and 15 minutes.
- Public Rust and Candid APIs, CLI and JSON shapes, stable schemas, allocation
  IDs, timers, cleanup batch size, dependencies, and capacity limits do not
  change.
- Stable eligibility semantics do change. Old terminal application rows are
  deliberately rejected rather than preserved or rewritten; zero such rows is
  the upgrade precondition.
- Cargo package versions remain untouched; `0.96.8` is the open changelog
  draft until the maintainer runs the release flow.
- The stale local Toko checkout is historical evidence only and was not
  inspected for this decision.

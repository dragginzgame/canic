## Cases Executed

All 12 `CANIC-094-B04` cases ran at acknowledged process-death barriers before
rename and after rename plus parent-directory synchronization for:

- `Stop`;
- `CreateSnapshot`;
- `Start`;
- `DownloadSnapshot`;
- `VerifyArtifact`; and
- `FinalizeManifest`.

The fixture executes every required predecessor through the maintained backup
runner, then kills a child while the selected operation's pending claim is
published. Every case is bound to its exact frozen manifest identity.

## Results

When the pending claim did not survive rename, restart observed `Ready` and
executed exactly the selected operation. Each run appended one matching
terminal receipt. Command-backed operations invoked exactly one expected
executor method; verification and finalization invoked none.

When the pending claim survived:

- stop observed the exact target as `Running`, issued one justified stop, and
  completed once;
- snapshot create, start, and download returned the typed
  `CommandOutcomeUnknown` cause, invoked no effect method, and preserved the
  pending journal plus receipt set exactly; and
- checksum verification and manifest finalization resumed through their
  existing exact local state machines, completed once, and appended one
  matching terminal receipt without an external command.

Result: all 12 `B04` cases pass. `CANIC-094-J02` remains pending.

## Finding And Fix

### `CANIC-094-BACKUP-001` - P1 - fixed

The generic runner recognized a pending operation but then unconditionally
called the ready-to-pending transition again. Command-backed operations
returned their typed unknown-outcome cause before that transition, but pending
`VerifyArtifact` and `FinalizeManifest` reached it and failed permanently with
an invalid transition on every restart.

The runner now writes a pending claim only for `Ready` or retryable `Failed`
work. An already-pending operation first applies its existing operation policy:
command-backed effects apply their exact operation policy, while the two local
operations resume without a second claim. The stop policy was added and rerun
under `B05`; the other three command-backed effects still halt as unknown. No
new state, recovery command, fallback, or compatibility path was added.

## Surface And Scope

- Persisted plan, journal, receipt, artifact, and manifest shapes are
  unchanged.
- At the initial B04 result, public Rust APIs, CLI/JSON output, Candid, and
  Cargo package versions were unchanged. The subsequent B05 slice in the same
  patch draft hard-cuts the injected Rust executor trait to require typed
  status observation.
- The test uses the shared typed executor and the existing test-only durable
  write barrier. It adds no production failpoint or hidden operator surface.
- Effect-specific create/start/download reconciliation remains assigned to
  later frozen cases; `B04` does not treat quiescence as proof of outcome.

## Finding

### `CANIC-094-RESTORE-003` — P2 — fixed

The private staged upload artifact was owned by a destructor during an
ordinary process lifetime. If upload succeeded and the runner died before its
receipt, restart reconciled the uploaded snapshot without constructing that
owner. If death occurred after terminal persistence but before destructor
execution, completed restart skipped the operation entirely. Both paths could
therefore retain a full non-authoritative snapshot copy indefinitely.

The artifact owner now removes exact upload staging after the command returns
and before terminal publication, and also on committed-upload reconciliation.
Cleanup validates the private stage root and operation entry before removal;
unsafe types fail closed. The journal remains `Pending` if cleanup itself
fails, preserving the authoritative recovery path.

## Recovery Evidence

Before a pending-claim write, the prior `Ready` journal survives byte for byte.
After the synchronized write, `Pending` survives with no new receipt and upload
retains its exact pre-effect inventory. Restart then uses the existing policy:

- upload compares the exclusive snapshot inventory;
- stop and start observe exact target status;
- load repeats the exact uploaded snapshot only while stopped; and
- verification repeats read-only structured status validation.

Terminal state and its receipt are serialized in one journal replacement.
Before the terminal rename, restart sees `Pending` with no receipt and
reconciles. After rename and parent-directory synchronization, restart sees
`Completed` with exactly one matching receipt and executes no command. Exact
load is the only mutating operation replayed after the pre-write crash, as
required by its proven stopped-target capability.

The final-response child reaches its barrier only after the completed response
exists in memory. Killing it loses only that response. Restart returns complete
with zero executed operations and an unchanged journal.

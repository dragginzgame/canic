branches has direct regression proof, recorded separately as the P2 evidence

## Findings

### High - root issuer policy has competing admission owners

Canonical finding: `CANIC-092-LAYERING-005` (`product_defect`, P1,
confirmed, open).

Evidence:

- `ops/auth/delegation/root_issuer_policy.rs:18-26` validates, constructs, and
  persists the issuer policy in one ops path.
- Its private validator at lines 69-92 owns max-TTL, refresh-ratio, enabled
  audience, and enabled grant admission decisions.
- `domain/policy/pure/auth/root_provisioning.rs:173-206` later re-evaluates
  issuer enablement, audience, grants, TTL, and the same refresh-ratio bounds.
- The exact `0 < refresh_after_ratio_bps < 10_000` rule appears in both ops
  lines 77-80 and policy lines 279-287.
- Ops imports and constructs policy-owned `RootIssuerPolicy`, then storage ops
  map it to persisted records. This is part of the broader 25-file upward edge
  already proven by module-structure v1.

Impact:

- A future rule change can make upsert admission disagree with later proof
  preparation or renewal.
- Upsert failures use broad `InternalError::invalid_input` strings while the
  later policy path has typed `AuthPolicyError` causes.
- Persistence occurs before any canonical policy-owned admission result, so
  the layer named as policy is not the sole decision authority.

Recommended consolidation:

- Resolve through the eventual `CANIC-092-LAYERING-005` slice: put persisted
  policy state under the canonical model owner, keep one pure admission
  decision, and leave ops responsible for conversion and bounded mutation.
- Do not create a wrapper or compatibility validator; delete the competing
  path when that finding is approved for implementation.

Deduplication decision: no new duplication ID. The canonical owner and
invariant are identical to `CANIC-092-LAYERING-005`; this report is an
additional discovery source.

### Medium - issuer-policy admission rejection paths lack direct proof

Canonical finding: `CANIC-092-TEST-001` (`evidence_gap`, P2, confirmed, open).

Evidence:

- The four ops rejection branches cover zero max TTL, invalid refresh ratio,
  enabled policy with no audience, and enabled policy with no grant.
- A repository-wide source/test scan found no call constructing
  `RootIssuerPolicyUpsertRequest` outside its DTO definition and no assertion
  for those four diagnostics or typed outcomes.
- Eight focused pure-policy tests pass, including invalid proof TTL and
  refresh-policy cases, but they do not execute the ops upsert validator or
  prove that rejected upserts leave policy state and registry epoch unchanged.

Impact:

- The duplicated boundary can drift or mutate state on rejection without a
  focused regression signal.
- Positive root provisioning integration tests do not cover these admission
  boundaries.

Recommended proof:

- In the eventual owner-consolidation slice, test the canonical pure decision
  and the mutation boundary for all four rejection classes, including
  unchanged persisted policy and epoch.
- Assert typed causes and observable state rather than display strings.

### Watchpoints - large private hubs and local parsers

- `scaffold/mod.rs`, `inspect/mod.rs`, and `auth/mod.rs` are private patch-radius
  hotspots, but the scans did not find a second command-family owner.
- Auth, blob-storage, and cycles each retain a small `parse_json_response` plus
  optional-wrapper helpers. They share host numeric/field primitives but map
  failures to different domain errors and accept different response shapes.
  Consolidating them now would hide command-specific contracts.
- Several stdout-only commands serialize directly because they expose no
  output-file mode. File-capable commands use `canic-cli::output`; no competing
  durable-write path was found.
- Backup/restore tests keep local fixtures close to phase-specific invariants.
  The persisted manifest, journal, receipt, and integrity logic remains owned
  by `canic-backup` production modules.
- Retained install/package proof scripts repeat temporary-root setup, but each
  proves a distinct installed or packaged release question. A mega-script
  would obscure those proof boundaries.

## Positive Consolidation

- Registry parsing, local query transport, and local-versus-ICP selection are
  separate, composable host owners.
- Shared response primitives remain host-owned while domain codecs preserve
  typed command context.
- All file-capable CLI report paths inspected route through the durable output
  helper; the focused serialization-failure test proves no partial overwrite.
- Stable evidence envelope types, schema references, fingerprints, hashing,
  summary precedence, and exit classes remain host-owned.
- Backup persistence validates before publication and owns integrity checks
  for plan, manifest, journal, receipts, topology, paths, and checksums.
- Root proof batch DTOs, stable records, mapper types, workflow sequencing,
  and endpoint guards are distinct layer representations rather than copied
  runtime workflows. The issuer-policy admission exception is isolated above.

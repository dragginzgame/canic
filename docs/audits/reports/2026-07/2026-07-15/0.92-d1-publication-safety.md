One root publication workflow now reserves durable-publication quota and cycle
headroom before bootstrap, controller-requested publication, or upgrade
reconciliation can reach store or management effects. The unforgeable permit
is carried through placement, store creation, inventory, store calls,
management chunk work, promotion, and root mirroring. Failed work commits the
quota use while releasing the cycle reservation; successful work settles both
intents.

Publication conflicts, capacity exhaustion, missing exact releases, chunk hash
and index failures, invalid state, missing stores, and unavailable transports
now retain distinct typed causes and stable public error codes. Store binding
and garbage-collection commands remain outside the publication cost boundary,
as required by D1 non-scope.
